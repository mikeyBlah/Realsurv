PROJECTS

Civil:Greater North Level Crossing Removals (Melbourne, VIC)

Client: John Holland
Status: Complete September 2016
Project Value: $530m

• Four grade separations achieved by excavating and sinking the existing rail by up to 7m to pass under newly constructed road bridges.

•Unprecedented ‘37-day construction blitz’ was undertaken for the three level crossings at Greater North whereby an additional 25-30 surveyors from external contractors were sourced to compliment an existing 1000 personal work crew working a 24/7 hour operation, for thirty seven (37) days straight.

•This was planned and designed to reduce what normally would have taken eighteen (18) months with conventional construction methodology.

•Scope required bringing in an additional 35 surveyors over a 24/7, hour day and night operation until project completion and rail corridor was re-opened.

Level Crossing Construction Blitz Summary:

• Machinery used continuously during blitz: 50 Excavators, 15 Dozers, 6 Graders and 6 loaders.

• Personnel: 1000 people on site each shift.

• Excavation: 260,000 cubic meters (that’s enough to fill the MCG), excavated and removed from site in ten (10) days.

• Key Statistic: Largest continuous rail shut down for any project since the construction of the Melbourne underground rail network in the 80’s.

Port Botany International Container Terminal 3 Upgrade (Port Botany, Sydney NSW)

Client: Laing O’Rourke
Status: Complete June 2014

• The new section of the Sydney International Container Terminal was constructed on 46Ha of reclaimed land and consisted of 100,000m3 of concrete paving (RMS specifications).

The project also included;

• Rail and associated concrete structures to support multiple automated rail stacking crane systems

• 42kms underground services (high & low voltage, communications, fire, sewer and water)

• Over 11 km of underground drainage

• 200,000m3 of rigid pavements

• Rail siding and existing rail tie-in works

Great Eastern Hwy Upgrade (Perth W.A)

Client: Leighton Contractors (CPB)
Status: Complete 2012
Project Value: $250m

• 4.2km highway widening, from four to six lanes

• 3.6km erection of segmented, prefabricated concrete noise wall system

• A central median with U-turn facilities at key locations

• On-road cycling facilities and continuous pedestrian paths

• Highway standard lighting

• Noise mitigation facilities for some adjacent residences

• An intelligent transport system comprising optic fibre communications to Main Roads Traffic Operations Centre CCTV
